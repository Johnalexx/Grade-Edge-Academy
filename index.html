    ABOUT GRADE EDGE ACADEMY

    Grade Edge Academy is a a value-driven, social innovation academy established in 2023 with a vision to empower the next generation of leaders from underserved communities in Africa through transformative STEM education and mentorship, ensuring that every child, regardless of background, has the opportunity to pursue a successful career and make a positive impact in his/her community and beyond.
    For us, diversity is a virtue in STEM and we are extraordinarily passionate about helping as many of our young girls as possible, to gain an interest, to maintain an interest and ultimately, to pursue a career in STEM. We know from our past success stories that a career in STEM for girls is fun, very rewarding, and can make a huge difference in breaking all cultural and societal stereotypes and biases that prevent them from reaching their goals.

    WHY STEM FOR GIRLS?

    • • According to UNESCO, only 35% of students in higher education globally are women.
    • • The development of STEM education is critical in achieving the objectives outlined by the United Nations in its 2030 Agenda and the Sustainable Development Goals, notably SDGs 4 and 5.




    Purpose Statement

    Our purpose is to address the lack of equal access to STEM education, mentorship and career opportunities for girls aged 12-14 in two underserved junior secondary schools in Idi-Araba Community, Mushin Lagos, Nigeria.

    Ambition Statement

    The ambition of our project is to launch a STEM club that will inspire and empower school girls to explore and pursue careers in STEM fields and to bridge the gender gap in STEM in two underserved junior secondary schools in Idi-Araba, Mushin, by the end of June 2024.

    Achievements & Awards

    Our partnership with PlannedPath Consults allows us to train science and mathematics teachers in secondary schools. We recently received an award from the Tutor-General/Permanent Secretary, Education District VI, Dr. (Mrs) Shareefah Ajoke Yusuf, for facilitating a capacity development workshop tagged "Education Reform and Innovation Training (ERIT)" for over 3,000 teachers in the district. We have also completed several professional courses with reputable local and international organizations. They include: SGs and the Law (University of Cambridge), Climate and Sustainability Job Simulation (Forage), SAIL Teachers' Fellowship, Teach4SDGs (AIIDEV Africa), Global STEM Educators, Migrant Project (SEEFAR), just to mention a few.
